Our Mission

The average cost of college per student lies at a whopping $35,000 per year. This is more than half of the national average salary of $53,490. With high demands pushing the inflation up to 10.8% in foods between 4/2021-4/2022, food insecurity is only becoming more prevalent among college students.

Statistical Facts

Here are some statistics from very recent surveys related to college students. The AACU revealed in 2018 that 55% of college sudents are financially independent, adding a load of financial stress.

Did you know that...

An average college meal plan costs about $4,500 per year

Estimated to approx. $18.75 per day, a day of food in college costs $7.00 more than the national average in US food cost per day.

Currently, 48% of community college students and 41% of 4-year college students report experiencing food insecurity

Higher than expected right? Studies show that nearly 70% of college students feel constantly stressed about their finances. Furthermore, 32% of students reported neglecting their studies at sometime because of the money owed.

Over one-third of students have experienced food insecurity since the COVID pandemic

The pandemic caused a world-wide economic fallout, leaving millions unemployed and prices skyrocketing. Being left with difficulties from inability to pay rents to expenses, many were left with food insecurity problems.

Lasting Effects of Food Insecurity

While studies confirm that food insecurity is linked to lower GPAs and lower academic performance, food-insecure college students are more likely to develop serious mental health issues". Mild to moderate psychological distress was higher among those who reported to being food insecure, in addition to ~60% of students feeling overwhelmed by schooling. This leads to an increase in anxiety and depression, lower GPA and academic performance, significantly higher dropout rates and increased debt.
